**The Problem of Collusion**

This leads to a phenomenon known as "collusion," where bidders engage in strategic behavior that benefits themselves at the expense of others.

**The Emergence of Collusion in Dynamic Optimization**

In dynamic optimization settings, collusion can arise due to various factors, including:

1. **Non-stationarity**: Bidders may update their bids based on new information or changes in market conditions, making it challenging for auction theory models to accurately predict their behavior.
2. **Uncertainty**: Real-world scenarios often involve uncertainty about the optimal bidding strategy, which can lead to collusion if bidders are not aware of the potential consequences of their actions.
3. **Strategic interactions**: Bidders may engage in strategic interactions with each other, such as bidding on similar items or competing for limited resources. These interactions can create a sense of mutual benefit, leading to collusion.
4. **Bidding strategies**: Bidders may use different bidding strategies, such as "bidding up" or "bidding down," which can lead to collusion if they are not aware of the other bidders' strategies.
